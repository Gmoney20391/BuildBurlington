Sign the Petition

Join your neighbors in calling for common-sense housing solutions.

We call on Burlington City Council to:

  • Allow duplexes, triplexes, and fourplexes in residential neighborhoods
  • Streamline approval processes for missing middle housing
  • Update zoning bylaws to support diverse housing options
  • Prioritize affordable housing initiatives in the city plan

By signing, you agree to receive emails from Build Burlington about this campaign and others. You can unsubscribe at any time.

Contact City Council

Personal messages to council members have the most impact.

Message Template

Dear Mayor [Name] and City Council members,

I'm writing to express my strong support for allowing missing middle housing options in Burlington's residential neighborhoods.

As a resident, I've seen firsthand how our housing crisis is affecting our community. Allowing duplexes, triplexes, and fourplexes in single-family neighborhoods would create more affordable housing options while preserving neighborhood character.

I urge you to support zoning reforms that would make this possible. Thank you for considering my perspective on this important issue.

Sincerely,
[Your Name]
Burlington Resident

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about taking action for housing solutions.

Will my personal information be shared?

We will never share your personal information without your permission. When you contact city council, only your name and message will be sent. We will not sell or distribute your contact information to third parties.

How often will I receive emails?

We typically send 1-2 emails per month with updates on the campaign and opportunities to take action. You can unsubscribe at any time using the link in any email.

What happens after I sign the petition?

We will present the petition to Burlington City Council at an upcoming meeting. We'll also keep you updated on the progress of the campaign and let you know about other ways to get involved.

Can I help in other ways?

Absolutely! You can help by talking to your neighbors about the issue, sharing information on social media, attending city council meetings, or volunteering with our organization. Contact us to learn more.
