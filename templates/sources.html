Data Sources

Transparent and verifiable data is essential for informed policy decisions. All data presented on this website comes from reputable public sources. Some data is estimated if there is no data available. Estimated data is never used to prove a trend only to fill in the gaps to give users a full picture.

Canada Mortgage and Housing Corporation (CMHC)

The CMHC provides comprehensive data on housing markets across Canada, including rental market surveys and housing starts/completions data.

Datasets provided:

  • Rental market statistics
  • Housing starts and completions
  • Vacancy rate data
  • Average rent prices
Visit CMHC website →

Statistics Canada

Canada's national statistical agency provides data on population, income, and economic indicators that are essential for housing analysis.

Datasets provided:

  • Census population data
  • Household income statistics
  • Demographic information
  • Economic indicators
Visit Statistics Canada →

City of Burlington Reports

Official publications and reports from the City of Burlington provide local context and specific municipal data.

Datasets provided:

  • Local housing strategy documents
  • Official plan and zoning data
  • Municipal demographic reports
  • Housing needs assessments
Visit City of Burlington →

Teranet-National Bank House Price Index

Provides reliable data on housing price trends across Canadian municipalities.

Datasets provided:

  • Historical home price data
  • Price index comparisons
  • Market trend analysis
Visit House Price Index →

Methodology Note

All data analysis follows standard statistical practices. Where data from different sources are combined or compared, adjustments have been made for consistency in:

  • Inflation adjustment to 2024 dollars
  • Standardized geographic boundaries
  • Consistent time periods for comparison
  • Per capita calculations using consistent population data

For specific methodological questions, please contact our research team.
